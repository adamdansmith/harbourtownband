About South Parade

Made in six days, shaped by Southsea

Although there was no single definite theme to the album, several tracks reflect our closeness to Portsmouth’s harbour and the seaside, and Jon’s artwork complements that perfectly.

We recorded the album with Neil Elliott at the Old Blacksmiths Studio and Southsea Sound Studio in just six days. With seven musicians, varied instrumentation, and eleven tracks to capture, that was a testament to Neil’s skill, patience, and work ethic, as well as the band’s willingness to deliver strong performances.

11 tracks · 7 musicians · recorded in 6 days

We had a lot of fun making the album and felt privileged to be the final band to record at Blacksmiths’. For most of us, this was the third album made there, and it has always been a pleasure.

Overall, the thoughts and complaints of a 72-year-old Brummie often find their way into the lyrics, but we hope you will also find hope, warmth, and joy there too.

We hope you enjoy the music — and that you come to see us when we are performing.
Hearing is believing.
